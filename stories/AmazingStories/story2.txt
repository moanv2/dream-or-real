Title: After 20+ years people still remember.

I was going to the bank to get some money for our daily expenditures - (what little I have in my account) when the security guard came up to me and said "Hey aren't you the child of Mrs. _______?" and I said yes I am.

He then proceeded to tell me what a saint and beautiful person my mom was. He was a security guard at the school my mom used to teach at. He continued to tell me stories of what my mom used to do for him and the other guards as well as other employees, little acts of kindness, a smile and greeting every time she saw them. Sharing her food and sometimes eating with them and other little things which made a great impact to this man.

My mother had passed away several years ago and he still remembered her fondly.

Over the years, after I have met other people who have shared similar stories, things I did not know about my mother or how she was with all these other people. A story which stood out was when her friend and co-worker was in the library and everything was quiet, nobody was there so the friend started singing and dancing while seated to her favorite songs - you know like nobody is watching. Then later on she glimpsed at the corner of her eye - my mother was seated in the far corner - just quietly working and then when she saw that her co-worker was looking at her in a daze and bit of nervous shame - my mom simply smiled in a way that she usually does which just calmed her and made her happy (the co-worker that is) and then my mom continued to work in silence.

So many other stories, acts of unselfishness and love... it makes me cry to this day. I wish she were still here - still here to make this crazy world a better place for all the people she comes across.
